## Deck-Level Focus

Uses examples to critique effective, misleading, cluttered, or unconventional visualization choices.

## Core Knowledge

- A chart can be visually appealing but analytically weak if it hides comparison or context.
- Bad visualizations often misuse scale, color, area, 3D effects, or chart type.
- Weird or novel visualizations require extra justification because unfamiliar encodings increase
    interpretation effort.
- Evaluation should ask whether the chart supports the analytical task clearly.

## How This Supports the Churn Report

- Use this deck as a quality-control checklist before finalizing churn charts.
- Reject chart choices that make churn-risk comparison harder even if they look decorative.
